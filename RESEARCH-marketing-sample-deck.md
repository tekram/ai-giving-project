# Marketing Sample Deck — Insights & Ideas

## Context

This deck belongs to a large-scale US-based Islamic nonprofit with a national chapter structure and $25M+ in total annual revenue (2025). The organization runs sophisticated multi-channel fundraising — Ramadan campaigns, corporate partnerships, chapter-based events, digital ads — and tracks performance across segmented donor tiers. Despite strong top-line growth ($18.79M → $22.45M → $25.20M from 2023–2025), several structural cracks are visible under the surface that givestack is well-positioned to address.

---

## Key Insights from the Slides

### 1. Level A Donors ($25K+) Collapsed in 2025 — The Silent Crisis
- Level A generated **$11.82M in 2024** but dropped to **$5.46M in 2025** — a $6.4M decline in a single tier
- This is circled in the deck, signaling leadership is aware but the root cause isn't addressed
- All other tiers (B through E) are relatively stable or growing
- **Insight:** The org is growing total revenue only because offline/chapter growth is masking a catastrophic major donor loss. If Level A doesn't recover, the 2026 trajectory reverses.

### 2. Online Share of Revenue Is Actually Shrinking Despite Digital Investment
- Online as % of total: 39% (2023) → 38% (2024) → **36% (2025)**
- Digital ad spend grew from $45K (2019) to $282K (2025) — a 516% increase
- Yet online revenue grew $2.14M → $6.13M (+186%) while total revenue grew faster offline
- **Insight:** The digital channel is efficient (3.7x ROAS) but not keeping pace with offline growth. Offline (events, chapters, direct mail) is the actual growth engine — not digital.

### 3. Ramadan Is 32%+ of Total Online Revenue in a Single Campaign Window
- Ramadan 2026 delivered **$8.1M (+11% YoY)** with 36% online
- This means one campaign window drives ~$2.9M of the $6.1M annual online total
- **Insight:** Extreme revenue concentration risk. A single bad Ramadan (geo-political event, competitor campaign, platform issue) wipes out a third of annual online income.

### 4. Average Gift Per Online Donor Is Declining — More Donors, Smaller Checks
- Avg per donor: $984 (2022) → $1,082 (2023) → **$939 (2024)**
- Donor count growing: 5,809 → 5,684 → 6,528
- More donors, smaller average — suggests acquisition is skewing toward lower-value segments
- **Insight:** Growth strategy is widening the funnel but not deepening it. The platform needs a tier upgrade path, not just acquisition.

### 5. Churn Is Improving But Still Structurally Dangerous
- Churn: 66% (2022) → 60% (2023) → **54% (2024)**
- Positive trend, but 54% churn means over half of all online donors never give again
- Retention on the SimpleKPI dashboard shows 88.93% — the discrepancy suggests different definitions (likely 88.93% applies to recurring/monthly donors only)
- **Insight:** Two different retention stories are being told to two audiences. The 54% figure is the real one for strategic planning.

### 6. Chapter Revenue Disparity Is Extreme — Top 5 Carry the Org
- Seattle $1.92M, DC $1.80M, Houston $1.43M, SF Bay Area $1.40M, Georgia $1.27M
- San Antonio: $45.94K — 42x gap between top and bottom chapter
- **Insight:** The top 5 chapters are doing something the bottom 30 are not. Playbook transfer from high-performing to low-performing chapters is the highest-leverage geographic growth opportunity.

### 7. Corporate Partners Are Disproportionately Valuable
- 20 corporate partners generating $2.5M = **$75.8K average partner value**
- Public donors: 61,255 donations = $18.28 average
- 20 partners deliver more than the public channel at 3,000x the per-donor value
- **Insight:** Corporate partnership is severely underinvested at only 20 partners. Even 10 additional partners at the current average adds $758K annually.

### 8. KPI Dashboard Shows 3 Broken Activities — No One Is Fixing Them
- Landing page optimization: **11% complete, progress halted**
- Influencer marketing: **4% complete, progress halted**
- Email campaigns: **31% complete, at risk**
- **Insight:** These are not minor gaps — email and landing pages are core conversion infrastructure. Halted at <15% suggests a resourcing or prioritization failure, not a strategy failure.

### 9. Online Revenue Is Underpowered Relative to Digital Spend
- $282K digital spend → $6.1M online revenue = 21.7x gross return
- But this includes organic, direct, social, email, and Feeling Blessed — paid ads are a subset
- Paid ROAS is 3.7x, which means non-paid channels (Ramadan, email, organic) carry most of the load
- **Insight:** Paid digital is actually a small driver of total online revenue. Ramadan campaign, email list, and peer-to-peer (Feeling Blessed/P2P) are the real online engines.

### 10. Dhul Hajj Is Named as the Next Major Campaign — But No Metrics Shown
- Executive summary lists "Dhul Hajj and monthly giving campaigns" as the near-term focus
- Ramadan is $8.1M; no comparable Dhul Hajj baseline is shown
- **Insight:** Dhul Hajj (Qurbani season) is the second-largest Islamic giving moment but appears underbuilt vs. Ramadan. This is a replicable campaign framework opportunity.

## Ideas for givestack

### Idea 1: Donor Tier Intelligence Dashboard
The 5-tier donor classification (Level A–E) with 3-year revenue trend is exactly what givestack's DonorIntelligence component should surface. Ideas:
- Visual tier breakdown with year-over-year revenue per tier
- Alert when a tier drops significantly (Level A alarm would have fired in early 2025)
- Upgrade path recommendations: "X donors in Level D gave 3+ times — prompt for Level C upgrade"
- Tier velocity tracking: how many donors moved up vs. down vs. lapsed per quarter

### Idea 2: Chapter / Geographic Revenue Benchmarking
The chapter revenue slide ($1.92M Seattle vs $45.94K San Antonio) reveals no playbook transfer mechanism. givestack could:
- Add geographic performance view to the NGO dashboard — revenue by city/chapter
- Surface top-performing chapter tactics as templates for lower-performing ones
- Identify under-penetrated cities relative to Muslim population density (Houston is #3 but could be higher)
- Flag chapters where digital as % of revenue is below average — those are digital investment opportunities

### Idea 3: Revenue Concentration Risk Scoring
Ramadan = ~32% of online revenue in one window. givestack could:
- Calculate a "campaign concentration score" for each NGO — what % of annual revenue comes from the top campaign
- Flag when concentration exceeds a threshold (e.g., >25% from one campaign)
- Suggest counter-programming: monthly giving push in off-peak months to flatten the curve
- Show what revenue would look like if Ramadan dropped 20% (scenario modeling)

### Idea 4: Islamic Giving Calendar — Ramadan + Dhul Hajj Twin-Peak Model
Ramadan is built. Dhul Hajj is the opportunity. givestack could:
- Build a two-peak giving calendar template for Islamic NGOs (Ramadan March-April, Dhul Hajj June-July)
- Pre-campaign engagement sequence for Dhul Hajj mirroring the Ramadan playbook
- Qurbani-specific donation flows (donor selects animal type/region — highly engaging for this audience)
- Year-round Islamic engagement calendar: Muharram, Mawlid, Shaban appeal for monthly giving activation

### Idea 5: Corporate Partnership CRM Module
20 partners at $75.8K average is severely underscaled. givestack could build:
- A "Corporate Partners" pipeline view in the NGO dashboard (currently companies are just donors, not relationship-managed)
- Partnership tier cards: Bronze/Silver/Gold with specific recognition benefits
- Matching gift activation: surface to donors that their employer matches — automated at donation checkout
- Annual renewal reminders and stewardship touchpoints for corporate accounts

### Idea 6: Chapter Playbook Transfer (High → Low Performer)
The 42x gap between top and bottom chapter is a playbook problem, not a potential problem. Ideas:
- "Chapter Health Score" surfaced in the NGO dashboard based on per-capita giving, digital %, churn rate
- Best practice templates from top-performing chapters: campaign timing, event types, solicitation cadence
- Peer comparison: "Houston raised $1.43M — your city has similar demographics but raised $210K"

### Idea 7: KPI Activity Tracker with Accountability Layer
The marketing KPI dashboard (Slide 2) shows 3 halted/at-risk activities with no escalation mechanism. givestack could:
- Add a marketing activity tracker to the NGO portal with progress, owner, deadline, status
- Auto-flag activities that go stale (no update in 14 days) or drop below threshold progress
- Surface blocked activities to leadership automatically rather than requiring manual deck review
- Connect campaign KPIs (email open rate, landing page conversion) to donation outcomes

### Idea 8: Online vs. Offline Attribution Dashboard
Online share is shrinking (39%→36%) while offline grows — but the org may not realize offline is the growth driver. givestack could:
- Show online vs. offline revenue split with trend line prominently in the NGO dashboard
- Attribution tagging: which offline events / chapters / mailings drove online follow-up donations?
- Help NGOs understand whether digital spend is actually acquiring new donors or just recapturing donors who would have given anyway

## Key Difference vs. OBAT Helpers

OBAT is a small NGO ($1.59M) with a churn and first-time donor problem. This org is a large NGO ($25M+) with a major donor concentration problem, a chapter scaling problem, and a digital efficiency plateau. The platform needs different tools for each:

- **OBAT**: post-donation onboarding, first-time → recurring conversion, Zakat calculator
- **This org**: major donor early-warning, chapter benchmarking, corporate CRM, revenue diversification away from Ramadan concentration

Both share the Islamic giving calendar opportunity — that feature serves the full spectrum.
